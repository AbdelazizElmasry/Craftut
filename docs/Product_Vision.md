# Product Vision

## Origin

This project originated from a perceived market opportunity. The initiative was started by a founder who identified several barriers preventing wider adoption of 3D printing among children and non-technical users:

- Safety concerns
- Complex workflows
- Intimidating user experience
- Maintenance requirements
- Technical knowledge requirements
- Lack of educational integration

Craftut was created to address these challenges directly.

---

## Target Users

**Primary users:**

- Children aged 8+
- First-time users with no prior 3D printing experience

**Customers:**

- Parents
- Schools and educational institutions
- STEM centers
- Makerspaces

---

## Product Requirements

Major requirements included:

| Requirement | Rationale |
|-------------|-----------|
| Child safety | Primary constraint; drives enclosure, interlock, and thermal design |
| Ease of use | Reduces support burden and expands addressable market |
| Guided operation | Replaces expert-dependent workflows with step-by-step assistance |
| Intuitive interface | Enables unsupervised or minimally supervised use |
| Reduced maintenance complexity | Improves reliability in educational environments |
| Attractive product identity | Encourages engagement and reduces intimidation |
| Compact footprint | Fits home and classroom environments |
| Lightweight construction | Improves portability and shipping economics |
| Future connectivity support | Enables platform expansion without hardware replacement |
| Educational suitability | Aligns with curriculum integration and learning outcomes |

**Approximate targets (not validated as final specifications):**

- Build volume: 170 × 170 × 150 mm
- Weight target: approximately 8 kg
- Desired operational speed: 200–300 mm/s (target, not achieved)
- Noise target: approximately 50–55 dB (not formally validated)

&gt; These values represent design targets and current system parameters. They are not presented as certified or guaranteed specifications.

---

## Platform Vision

The printer is the entry point into a broader ecosystem:

1. **Hardware:** Child-safe, approachable 3D printer
2. **Software:** Connected ecosystem with guided workflows
3. **Content:** Educational learning pathways and structured projects
4. **Community:** Social features, sharing, and gamification
5. **Experiences:** Project kits and expanded STEM offerings

This ecosystem approach influenced architecture decisions, particularly around connectivity, controller separation, and user interface design.
